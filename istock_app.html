iStock iPad App

UX Design . Agile . DEV-Design pairing . 2013

While a designer on the mobile team at Getty Images, I lead the UX design effort for the new iStock iPad App. I lead a series of working sessions with product and development to determine the high level design concepts and feature set, and then produced the UX design based on that collaboration.

Getty Images iStock brand had a mobile app that was designed for phones, but using the app on a tablet was a poor experience. From previous user research we learned that our users preferred to use a tablet to browse and collect imagery. My challenge was to lead the UX design effort for the tablet experience and assist the visual designer in applying a visual language. The priorities for the app was to provide easy access to browsing, searching, saving, and sharing imagery.

The challenge

Before kicking off the project I worked with an researcher to gather data from current users of the iStock iPad app and potential users via a survey. When using a tablet, we discovered that our users primarily wanted to search for, browse, collect, and share images, so those formed our primary design goals.
We discovered that users prefered using a tablet to browse and collect imagery, which influenced our primary design goals
This helped us establish that the primary goals of the app design were to provide easy access to browsing, searching, saving, and sharing.

The design process unfolded in collaboration with development, utilizing a design approach that was based in loose concepts and sketches.

Understanding content

One of the main components of the iPad app was a dashboard that would surface content and help the user pick up where they left off. I brainstormed with other stakeholders using sticky notes and whiteboard to analyze what types of content could be surfaced.

Talk about brainstorming types of content to show for signed-in and signed-out users from the user dashboard.

For signed-in users we wanted to emphasize how many credits they had left on their account and provide quick access to recents and saved images.
For signed-out users we wanted to emphasize images from the iStock collection, especially most popular and curated collections.

Finalizing interactions

I started to move to digital when I needed to map all of the user scenarios and edge cases. Step by step I indicated how an user could move from one area to the next.
