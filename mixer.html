Getty Images Employee Portal

UX Design . Internal Tool . Cross Fuctional . 2014

The Getty Images employee portal, Mixer, needed a homepage and site navigation overhaul. I was tasked with designing the new user experience and applying a visual layer. This cross functional project needed a quick turn around with a design that could be built on a restrictive platform.

The challenge

The goals of the redesign were to increase employee engagement, provide relevant content, and to organize the homepage in a way that benefits all employees. This was also an opportunity to apply an updated digital brand. The old layout was heavily focused on one department, with relavant content hidden below complex navigation.

Gathering data

The first step was to understand how the site was currently organized and map out the various content areas. In addition, I wanted to collect any relevant data to help make informed design decisions.

You can often find my desk covered in sticky notes to help outline features and wrap my brain around content organization. In addition, a quick look at previous site analytics on the white board helps to map out user priorities and compare them to business goals. Itâ€™s useful to use sticky notes to map out content areas in a quick loose way and develop site hierarchy.

I gathered available user data on top hit pages and content areas on the previous site, communicating user priorities back to stakeholders to help inform business goals.
With business and user goals in place I began to brainstorm how content areas could be prioritized on the page. Sticky notes are always an important component of my workflow and they help breakdown complex information into digestable parts. At this phase it's also important to keep design concepts loose and high level to avoid fixating on visual details.

Developing the solution

The next step was to take the loose content sketches and tighten into wireframes. I built wireframes demonstrating several design directions with each emphasizing a different user priority.

The focus of this layout was around communication, featuring both external Getty Images related news, but also internal communications via Yammer. This designs also included a module for viewing the latest in Getty Images imagery.
This layout emphasized latest news and communication, but more equally featured employee resources like quick links or department areas.
The focus of this layout was visually communicating updates and news.It was a chance to more boldly showcase the content of the visual forward company of Getty Images.

Finalizing the design

The final direction emphasized educating employees about Getty Images news and community updates, while providing quick access to commonly used employee resources that were previously difficult to access. The final layout had some basic custumization with a "My Department" area with relatable links. Lastly, we incorporated a section to provide quick links to the many Getty Products. The new digital Getty brand was incorporated through typography and color.

Key take aways

This project successfully brought together various department heads, design, and development in a collaborative, cross functional way. In the end the restrictive platform was challenging to design an engaging experience. It proved that it's always valuable to engage dev early and often in the design process.
