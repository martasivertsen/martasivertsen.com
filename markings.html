Markings

While a student at Seattle Central College, I worked to develop a unique alphabet, attempting to push the boundary of how to define letter forms and typography. I collected interesting objects such as the teeth of a plastic comb or a happy birthday balloon.

Objects & their marks

I collected interesting objects such as the teeth of a plastic comb or a happy birthday balloon. With these objects and acrylic paint, I drew letters, discovering unique and unpredictable voices.

The balloon has no sharp edges making full and rounded marks that blended into eachother.
Hair takes paint well, like a standard paint brush, but the action of applying the paint needs to be have a more rolling emphasis in order to make legible marks.
The pine needles are thin and brittle, and it's necessary to lay them flat when making marks on the paper. Use several straight marks to approximate the curve of a "D".

Arranging into patterns

Manipulating these letters digitally and arranging them into patterns, helped further uncover the descriptive qualities of these forms, and the way the letters can communicate in other ways than just linguistically.

Capturing the process in book

I displayed the entire project in a book as an intimate way for the viewer to experience the different letters.
